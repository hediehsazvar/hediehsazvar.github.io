About

I am a PhD candidate in Operations Research at Northwestern University (IEMS). My research studies sequential matching and recommendation in online platforms under uncertainty (e.g., no-shows and overbooking), with an emphasis on scalable optimization algorithms and policy design.

I work across mixed-integer programming, stochastic dynamic programming, and simulation-based evaluation, motivated by healthcare staffing platforms (e.g., nurse-to-shift assignments).

Research

Sequential job recommendation under uncertainty

I study online platforms where agents arrive over time and must be recommended limited-capacity jobs. Each agent may decline or accept and later no-show; cancellations can render capacity unsellable. The objective is to maximize filled jobs and expected revenue while respecting capacity requirements.

  • Policy structure (nestedness, partially nested solutions)
  • Overbooking decisions to hedge no-shows
  • Preference models (independent and dependent choice)
  • Algorithmic approaches: MIP + heuristics + rollout/lookahead

Algorithms & implementation

My work emphasizes implementable, scalable methods for large problem sizes.

  • MIP formulations (linear/quadratic/polynomial) and solver tuning
  • Reformulations (e.g., quadratization, linearization, piecewise approximation)
  • Decomposition and cut generation
  • Simulation framework for policy evaluation

Tools: Python, Gurobi, AMPL, SQL/BigQuery, R.
