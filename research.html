Research

Sequential job recommendation under no-shows

I study online matching platforms where agents arrive sequentially and the platform recommends limited-capacity jobs under uncertain commitment (no-shows). My work characterizes the structure of optimal recommendation and overbooking policies and develops scalable optimization methods to maximize filled jobs and expected revenue.

Keywords: sequential decision-making · revenue management · no-shows · overbooking · large-scale optimization

Working papers

Optimal Sequential Job Recommendations with No-Shows
In preparation for submission to Manufacturing & Service Operations Management
Abstract. We study sequential job recommendation where agents arrive over time and may accept recommended jobs but later fail to show up. The platform must allocate limited capacity under uncertainty to maximize filled jobs and expected revenue. We analytically characterize optimal recommendation policies and develop scalable algorithms based on these structural insights.
Overbooking in Sequential Job Recommendation Platforms with No-Shows
In preparation for submission
Abstract. We study overbooking in sequential job recommendation platforms with no-show risk. We characterize optimal overbooking policies and develop scalable optimization methods to improve fill rates and expected revenue under capacity uncertainty.
Optimization Models and Reformulations for Job Recommendation with Dependent Preferences and No-Shows
Working paper
Abstract. We develop mixed-integer reformulations for high-dimensional 0–1 polynomial optimization problems arising in job recommendation systems with dependent preferences and no-show risk. We introduce scalable formulations and solver-oriented techniques to enable large-scale implementation.
Rollout-Augmented Lookahead Policies for Dynamic Join Decisions in Fork-Join Queues
In preparation for submission · PDF · Code
Abstract. We develop rollout-augmented lookahead policies for fork-join queueing systems. These policies approximate optimal dynamic programming solutions while remaining computationally scalable and implementable.
