Research

Sequential job recommendation under no-shows

I study online matching platforms where agents arrive sequentially and the platform recommends limited-capacity jobs under uncertain commitment (no-shows). My work characterizes the structure of optimal recommendation and overbooking policies and develops scalable optimization methods to maximize filled jobs and expected revenue.

Keywords: sequential decision-making · stochastic optimization · revenue management · no-shows · overbooking · 0–1 polynomial optimization · MIP reformulations · large-scale optimization · heuristics

Working papers

Optimal Sequential Job Recommendations with No-Shows
In preparation for submission to Manufacturing & Service Operations Management.

Abstract. The increasing reliance on online platforms to match job opportunities with agents highlights the need for job recommendation policies. We formulate the problem as a 0–1 polynomial program and characterize structural properties of optimal recommendation policies. We develop scalable algorithms that significantly improve expected revenue compared with standard approaches.

Overbooking in Sequential Job Recommendation Platforms with No-Shows
In preparation for submission.

Abstract. We extend the sequential recommendation framework to incorporate overbooking under no-show risk. We reformulate the resulting high-degree polynomial optimization problem and develop scalable algorithms. Computational results demonstrate substantial revenue improvements compared to non-overbooking policies.

Optimization Models and their Reformulations in Job Recommender System with Dependent Preferences and No-Shows.
Working paper.

Abstract. We study job recommendation under dependent preferences using discrete choice models and derive mixed-integer reformulations. We strengthen these formulations using valid inequalities and scalable optimization techniques, enabling efficient solution of large-scale instances.

Rollout-Augmented Lookahead Policies for Dynamic Join Decisions in Fork-Join Queues
In preparation for submission. Links: PDF · Code

Abstract. We develop rollout-augmented lookahead policies for fork-join queueing systems that approximate optimal dynamic programming performance while remaining computationally tractable. Results demonstrate meaningful cost reductions compared to baseline heuristics.
